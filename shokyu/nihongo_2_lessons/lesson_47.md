## Grammar Points

### 1. Plain form + そうです (Hearsay — "I heard that ~")

Reports information heard from someone else or from a source.

**Formation:** Plain form + そうです

> **Different from L43!** L43 そうです = appearance. L47 そうです = hearsay.

| L43 (Appearance) | L47 (Hearsay) |
|-------------------|---------------|
| おいし**そうです** (looks delicious) | おいしい**そうです** (I heard it's delicious) |
| 降り**そうです** (looks like it'll rain) | 降る**そうです** (I heard it'll rain) |

**Examples:**
- 田中さんは 来月 結婚する**そうです**。 — I heard Tanaka-san is getting married next month.
- 天気予報に よると、明日は 雨だ**そうです**。 — According to the forecast, it'll rain tomorrow.
- あの 映画は とても おもしろい**そうです**。 — I heard that movie is very interesting.
- 新しい 店は あまり おいしくない**そうです**。 — I heard the new restaurant isn't very good.

> Often used with **～によると** (according to ~).

---

### 2. V / adj / N + ようです (Conjecture based on evidence)

The speaker **infers/guesses** based on what they see, hear, or know. "It seems ~"

**Formation:**
- V plain + ようです
- い-adj + ようです
- な-adj + **な** ようです
- N + **の** ようです

**Examples:**
- 誰も いない ようです。 — It seems nobody is here.
- この 料理は 辛い ようです。 — This dish seems spicy.
- 彼は 病気の ようです。 — He seems to be sick.
- 事故が あった ようです。 — It seems there was an accident.
- 風邪を ひいた ようです。 — It seems I caught a cold.

---

### 3. ～そうです vs ～ようです (Key difference)

| ～そうです (hearsay) | ～ようです (conjecture) |
|---------------------|-----------------------|
| Based on what you **heard/read** | Based on what you **observed/inferred** |
| Source: other people, news, etc. | Source: your own senses/reasoning |

---

## Mini Quiz

**Q1.** Say "I heard the exam was difficult." (Use hearsay そうです)

**Q2.** Say "It seems like nobody is home." (Use ようです)

**Q3.** Say "According to the news, there was an earthquake." (Use によると + そうです)

**Q4.** Say "He seems tired." (Use ようです)

**Q5.** Say "I heard that restaurant is cheap." (Use hearsay そうです)

## Grammar Points

### 1. V て / い-adj くて / な-adj で / N で (Reason/Cause)

The て-form can express a reason or cause, especially for **emotions and involuntary states**.

**Examples:**
- ニュースを 聞いて、びっくりしました。 — I was surprised to hear the news.
- 試験に 受かって、うれしいです。 — I'm happy I passed the exam.
- 地震で ビルが 倒れました。 — The building collapsed due to the earthquake.
- 渋滞で 遅れました。 — I was late because of the traffic jam.

> Cannot be used for volitional actions in the second clause. (Use から/ので instead.)

---

### 2. ～ので (Because ~, polite reason)

Politer and softer than ～から. Preferred in formal/polite speech.

**Formation:** Plain form + ので (な-adj/Noun: ～な + ので)

**Examples:**
- 熱が ある**ので**、会社を 休みます。 — Because I have a fever, I'll take the day off.
- 道が わからなかった**ので**、タクシーに 乗りました。 — Because I didn't know the way, I took a taxi.
- 日曜日な**ので**、銀行は 休みです。 — Because it's Sunday, the bank is closed.
- 用事が ある**ので**、お先に 失礼します。 — I have things to do, so I'll excuse myself.

---

### 3. ～のに (Although ~ / Despite ~)

Expresses **contrast** — the result is unexpected or frustrating.

**Formation:** Plain form + のに (な-adj/Noun: ～な + のに)

**Examples:**
- 薬を 飲んだ**のに**、まだ 熱が あります。 — Although I took medicine, I still have a fever.
- 約束した**のに**、来ませんでした。 — Despite the promise, they didn't come.
- まだ 5月な**のに**、暑いですね。 — Even though it's only May, it's hot.
- たくさん 練習した**のに**、試合に 負けました。 — Despite practicing a lot, we lost the match.

> **のに** often carries a feeling of **frustration or regret**.

## Mini Quiz

**Q1.** Say "I was moved by the movie." (Use て for cause)

**Q2.** Say "Because the train stopped, I was late." (Use ので)

**Q3.** Say "Although I studied, I failed the test." (Use のに)

**Q4.** Say "I was relieved to hear the news." (Use て)

**Q5.** Say "Even though it's expensive, it's not good." (Use のに)

# 部首 (Bushu) — Japanese Kanji Radicals

> **Purpose:** Master the building blocks of Kanji. Knowing radicals helps you guess meanings, look up unknown Kanji, and memorize new characters faster.

---

## How Radicals Work

- Every Kanji is made of one or more **radicals** (部首 — bushu).
- The **semantic radical** often hints at the **meaning** category.
- The **phonetic component** often hints at the **reading**.
- Radicals can appear in different positions: left (偏 hen), right (旁 tsukuri), top (冠 kanmuri), bottom (脚 ashi), enclosure (構 kamae), or hanging (垂 tare).

### Radical Positions

| Position | Japanese | Location | Example |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|
| 偏 (へん) | hen | Left side | 言 → 話、語、読 |
| 旁 (つくり) | tsukuri | Right side | 刂 → 別、刊、判 |
| 冠 (かんむり) | kanmuri | Top | 宀 → 家、宝、室 |
| 脚 (あし) | ashi | Bottom | 灬 → 点、熱、煮 |
| 構 (かまえ) | kamae | Enclosure | 門 → 間、開、関 |
| 垂 (たれ) | tare | Top-left hanging | 广 → 店、広、庭 |
| 繞 (にょう) | nyō | Bottom-left wrap | 辶 → 道、近、通 |

## Study Tips

1. **Learn radicals before Kanji.** Recognizing radicals inside a Kanji makes it easier to remember and look up.
2. **Group by radical.** When you see 氵 on the left, expect the Kanji relates to water (海、湖、泳、洗、流).
3. **Use the position.** The meaning radical is usually on the left or top; the sound radical is usually on the right or bottom.
4. **Draw them.** Writing radicals by hand builds muscle memory and visual recognition.
5. **Make stories.** Combine radical meanings into a mini-story for each Kanji — this is the basis of mnemonic learning.

# JLPT N5 漢字 — Kanji Learning Guide

> **Level:** Beginner | **Total Kanji:** ~80 | **Goal:** Read basic sentences, signs, and simple texts
>
> This guide organizes N5 Kanji by **radical families** to reveal shared patterns, then groups them by **learning context** so you build real-world reading ability — not just isolated memorization.

---

## How to Use This Guide

1. **Study one section at a time.** Each section contains 5–15 Kanji grouped by a shared radical or theme.
2. **Learn the radical first.** Understanding the radical helps you decode new Kanji faster.
3. **Read vocabulary aloud.** This trains both recognition and pronunciation.
4. **Write each Kanji by hand** at least 5 times while saying the reading.
5. **Use the review section** at the end to test yourself after completing all sections.

---

## Section 1: Numbers — 数字（すうじ）

> **Radicals in focus:** 一 (one stroke), 口 (mouth), 十 (cross)
>
> Numbers are the first Kanji any learner should master. Notice how 一、二、三 are simply stroke counts.

---

### 一 — One

- **Onyomi:** イチ・イツ | **Kunyomi:** ひと（つ）
- **Radical:** 一 (one)
- **Vocabulary:**
  - 一つ（ひとつ）— one (thing)
  - 一人（ひとり）— one person / alone
  - 一月（いちがつ）— January
- **Example:** りんごを一つください。— Please give me one apple.
- **Mnemonic:** A single horizontal line — the simplest way to write "one."

---

### 二 — Two

- **Onyomi:** ニ | **Kunyomi:** ふた（つ）
- **Radical:** 二 (two)
- **Vocabulary:**
  - 二つ（ふたつ）— two (things)
  - 二人（ふたり）— two people
  - 二月（にがつ）— February
- **Example:** コーヒーを二つお願いします。— Two coffees, please.
- **Mnemonic:** Two horizontal lines stacked — twice the "one."

---

### 三 — Three

- **Onyomi:** サン | **Kunyomi:** み（つ）・みっ（つ）
- **Radical:** 一 (one)
- **Vocabulary:**
  - 三つ（みっつ）— three (things)
  - 三人（さんにん）— three people
  - 三月（さんがつ）— March
- **Example:** 三月に日本へ行きます。— I will go to Japan in March.
- **Mnemonic:** Three horizontal lines — the pattern of counting with strokes ends here.

---

### 四 — Four

## Section 3: Time & Calendar — 時間とカレンダー

> **Radicals in focus:** 日 (sun/day), 月 (moon), 人 (person), 寸 (measure)
>
> Time Kanji appear everywhere — on train schedules, invitations, and daily conversation.

---

### 年 — Year

- **Onyomi:** ネン | **Kunyomi:** とし
- **Radical:** 干 (dry)
- **Vocabulary:**
  - 今年（ことし）— this year
  - 去年（きょねん）— last year
  - 来年（らいねん）— next year
- **Example:** 今年、日本語を勉強し始めました。— I started studying Japanese this year.
- **Mnemonic:** A person carrying grain through the seasons — one full harvest cycle.

---

### 時 — Time / Hour

- **Onyomi:** ジ | **Kunyomi:** とき
- **Radical:** 日 (sun)
- **Vocabulary:**
  - 時間（じかん）— time / hour
  - 時計（とけい）— clock / watch
  - 一時（いちじ）— one o'clock
- **Example:** 何時ですか。— What time is it?
- **Mnemonic:** The sun 日 next to a temple 寺 — monks tell time by the sun at the temple.

---

### 分 — Minute / Part / Understand

- **Onyomi:** ブン・フン・プン | **Kunyomi:** わ（かる）・わ（ける）
- **Radical:** 刀 (sword/knife)

  - 自分（じぶん）— oneself
  - 分かる（わかる）— to understand
- **Example:** 五分待ってください。— Please wait five minutes.
- **Mnemonic:** A knife 刀 dividing something into parts — cutting time into minutes.

---

### 半 — Half

- **Onyomi:** ハン | **Kunyomi:** なか（ば）
- **Radical:** 十 (ten)
- **Vocabulary:**

---

---

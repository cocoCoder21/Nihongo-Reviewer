# JLPT N1 漢字 — Kanji Learning Guide

> **Level:** Advanced | **Prerequisite:** N5 + N4 + N3 + N2 mastery
>
> N1 represents native-level reading ability. These Kanji appear in literature, academic papers, and formal documents.

---

## How to Use This Guide

1. **Work through one section per study session.** Each section is themed so vocabulary reinforces naturally.
2. **Read example sentences aloud.** Context-based learning cements kanji faster than rote drilling.
3. **Test yourself with the review section** at the end after completing all 10 sections.
4. **Write each kanji by hand** — at N1 level, precision in stroke order distinguishes mastery from memorization.

### Study Tips for N1 Preparation

> **N1 is a marathon, not a sprint.** At this level, isolated flashcard drilling has diminishing returns. Instead:
>
> 1. **Read extensively in Japanese.** Newspapers (朝日新聞, NHK News Web), novels (村上春樹, 東野圭吾), and academic abstracts will expose you to N1 kanji in natural context.
> 2. **Watch formal content.** NHK documentaries, Diet session broadcasts (国会中継), and courtroom dramas use governance, legal, and formal vocabulary daily.
> 3. **Write by hand.** Many N1 kanji have 15+ strokes. Physical writing cements stroke order and reveals look-alike kanji you might confuse when only reading.
> 4. **Study kanji in compounds, not isolation.** A single kanji like 融 means little alone, but 金融・融資・融合 builds a web of meaning.
> 5. **Use the "1 section per week" rule.** Master 8 kanji deeply rather than skimming 80 kanji shallowly. Revisit previous sections every weekend.
> 6. **Practice the self-tests above without looking.** If you can produce the kanji from English and the meaning from kanji, you own it.

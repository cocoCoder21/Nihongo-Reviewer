## Grammar Points

### 1. ～ざるを得ない (Have no choice but to ~)

**Meaning:** Cannot help but ~ / Have no choice but to ~.

**Formation:** V ない stem + ざるを得ない (する → せざるを得ない)

**Examples:**
- 人手不足で、外国人を 採用せざるを得ない。 — Due to labor shortage, we have no choice but to hire foreigners.
- 予算の 関係で、計画を 変更せざるを得ませんでした。 — Due to budget, we had no choice but to change the plan.

---

### 2. ～を余儀なくされる (To be forced to ~)

**Meaning:** To be compelled/forced to ~ (by circumstances). Very formal.

**Formation:** N + を余儀なくされる

**Examples:**
- 災害で 多くの 人が 避難を余儀なくされました。 — Many people were forced to evacuate due to the disaster.
- 経営悪化で リストラを余儀なくされた。 — Due to worsening management, they were forced to lay off workers.

---

### 3. ～にもかかわらず (Despite ~ / In spite of ~)

**Meaning:** Despite ~ / Even though ~. More formal than のに.

**Formation:** N / V plain / adj + にもかかわらず

**Examples:**
- 対策を 立てたにもかかわらず、状況は 改善しませんでした。 — Despite taking measures, the situation didn't improve.
- 少子化が 深刻にもかかわらず、十分な 支援が ありません。 — Despite the serious declining birthrate, there isn't enough support.

---

### 4. ～一方だ (Keep ~-ing / Continually ~)

**Meaning:** Just keeps ~-ing. One-directional change (often negative).

**Formation:** V dictionary + 一方だ

**Examples:**
- 高齢者の 数は 増える一方です。 — The number of elderly people just keeps increasing.
- 地球の 気温は 上がる一方だ。 — Earth's temperature just keeps rising.
- 若者の 人口は 減る一方です。 — The youth population just keeps declining.

## Mini Quiz

**Q1.** Say "Due to the aging population, we have no choice but to reform the pension system" using ～ざるを得ない.

**Q2.** Express "Many workers were forced to change their work style" using ～を余儀なくされる.

**Q3.** Say "Despite economic growth, poverty is increasing" using ～にもかかわらず.

**Q4.** Express "The environment just keeps getting worse" using ～一方だ.

**Q5.** Describe a social issue: "Japan's declining birthrate and aging population have become serious problems."

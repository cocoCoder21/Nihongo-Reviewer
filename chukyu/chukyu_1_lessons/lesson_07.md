## Grammar Points

### 1. ～ものなら (If it were possible / If you dare)

**Meaning:** (a) If it were possible to ~ (wish). (b) If you dare ~ (warning).

**Formation:** V potential / V volitional + ものなら

**Examples:**
- 戻れるものなら、学生時代に 戻りたいです。 — If I could go back, I'd want to return to my student days.
- やれるものなら やってみろ。 — If you think you can, try it! (challenge)
- 時間が あるものなら、ゆっくり 話したいです。 — If I had time, I'd like to talk at length.

---

### 2. ～からには / ～以上は (Now that / Since ~ must)

**Meaning:** Now that ~ / Since ~ (implies obligation or strong commitment).

**Formation:** V plain / V た + からには / 以上は

**Examples:**
- 日本に 来たからには、日本語を マスターしたいです。 — Now that I've come to Japan, I want to master Japanese.
- 約束した以上は、守らなければなりません。 — Since you promised, you must keep it.
- やると 決めたからには、最後まで 頑張ります。 — Since I decided to do it, I'll do my best to the end.

---

### 3. ～つつ / ～つつも (While / Even though)

**Meaning:** ～つつ = while doing ~. ～つつも = even though ~, despite ~. (Written/formal)

**Formation:** V ます-stem + つつ / つつも

**Examples:**
- 体に 悪いと 知りつつ、タバコを やめられません。 — Even though I know it's bad for me, I can't quit smoking.
- 相手の 気持ちを 考えつつ、話すことが 大切です。 — It's important to speak while considering the other person's feelings.

---

### 4. ～とは限らない (Not necessarily)

**Meaning:** It's not necessarily the case that ~.

**Formation:** Plain form + とは限らない

**Examples:**
- 高ければ いいとは限りません。 — Expensive doesn't necessarily mean good.
- 日本人が みんな 本音を 言わないとは限りません。 — It's not necessarily true that all Japanese people hide their true feelings.

## Mini Quiz

**Q1.** Express a wish: "If I could speak Japanese fluently, I'd work in Japan" using ～ものなら.

**Q2.** Say "Now that I've started, I'll finish" using ～からには.

**Q3.** Say "Even though he knows it's wrong, he does it" using ～つつも.

**Q4.** Express "Silence doesn't necessarily mean agreement" using ～とは限らない.

**Q5.** Explain the concept of 本音と建前 in one sentence.

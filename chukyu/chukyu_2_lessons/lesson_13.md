## Grammar Points

### 1. ～させていただきます (Allow me to ~ — formal humble)

**Meaning:** Allow me to do ~. Used in business/formal settings to announce one's own action humbly.

**Formation:** V causative て + いただきます

**Examples:**
- それでは、説明させていただきます。 — Then, allow me to explain.
- 明日 休ませていただきます。 — I will take tomorrow off (with your permission).
- 資料を 送らせていただきます。 — Allow me to send you the materials.

---

### 2. ～ていただければ幸いです (I would be grateful if you could ~)

**Meaning:** It would be appreciated if you could ~. Very polite written request.

**Formation:** V て + いただければ 幸いです

**Examples:**
- ご確認いただければ幸いです。 — I would be grateful if you could confirm.
- ご検討いただければ幸いです。 — I would appreciate your consideration.

---

### 3. Keigo in context: 社内 vs. 社外

When speaking to someone outside your company about your own colleagues, use humble forms even for your boss.

**Examples:**
- (社外に) 山田は ただいま 外出しております。 — Yamada is currently out. (humble for own colleague)
- (社外に) 部長の 田中は 席を 外しております。 — Director Tanaka is away from his desk.
- (社内で 上司に) 部長、資料が できました。ご確認いただけますか。 — Director, the materials are ready. Could you check them?

---

### 4. ～かと思いますが / ～かと存じますが (I think ~ but / polite hedging)

**Meaning:** I think/believe ~, but… Softens assertions before making a request or counter-opinion.

**Formation:** Plain form + かと思いますが / かと存じますが

**Examples:**
- お忙しいかと思いますが、よろしくお願いいたします。 — I imagine you're busy, but I appreciate your help.
- ご存じかと思いますが、来月から 制度が 変わります。 — As you may know, the system changes from next month.

## Mini Quiz

**Q1.** Announce "Allow me to give a presentation" using ～させていただきます.

**Q2.** Write a polite email request: "I would be grateful if you could reply by Friday" using ～いただければ幸いです.

**Q3.** An outside client calls asking for your boss. Say "Director Suzuki is currently in a meeting" using appropriate keigo.

**Q4.** Soften a request: "I know you're busy, but could you check this?" using ～かと思いますが.

**Q5.** What is the difference between 尊敬語 and 謙譲語?
